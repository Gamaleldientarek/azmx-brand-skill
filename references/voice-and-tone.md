# AZMX Voice & Tone

How AZMX sounds, in English and Arabic. The visual system's ethos applies to words too: restraint is the luxury. Say less, mean more.

## The voice in one line

Confident, editorial, human, premium. Never templated, never corporate-stiff, never AI-generated in feel.

## Brand language

- **Tagline territory:** Forward · Human Always · Moving Ideas Forward.
- **Boilerplate:** AZMX is a Saudi UX and innovation design studio.
- **Bilingual brand:** copy is typically English, Arabic appears in logo lockups, emails, and market-facing work. Azm X carries both scripts. In Arabic emails, kashida-stretched wordmarks are copy-pasted, never retyped.

## Tone rules

1. **Confident, not boastful.** State what AZMX does plainly. Let the work carry the weight; skip superlatives like "world-class" and "cutting-edge."
2. **Editorial, not marketing-speak.** Write like a premium magazine, not a landing page. Short declarative sentences. Concrete nouns over abstractions.
3. **Human, always.** Warm and direct. Address the reader as a person. Contractions are fine in English. No jargon walls.
4. **Premium means quiet.** No exclamation marks doing the selling. No ALL-CAPS urgency. Scarcity of emphasis is what makes emphasis land, exactly like Electric blue on the page.

## Writing mechanics (the no-AI-tells rules)

Copy must read as written by a human, and an elite one. These are instant AI tells; never ship them:

- Em-dashes as the default connector. Use commas, periods, or a colon instead.
- Triads everywhere ("fast, simple, and powerful"). One strong claim beats three padded ones.
- Empty intensifiers: "truly," "seamlessly," "effortlessly," "robust," "leverage," "elevate," "unlock," "empower," "delve."
- Symmetrical paragraph lengths and mirrored sentence openers. Vary rhythm like a human editor would.
- Every sentence hedged ("can help," "may enable"). Commit: it does or it does not.
- Summaries that restate the heading. If the heading said it, the body adds something new or gets cut.

## By format

- **Headlines and heroes:** serif voice, short, mixed-case. A single sharp idea. No period at the end.
- **Eyebrows/labels:** UPPERCASE, 1 to 3 words, functional not clever.
- **Body copy:** sentence case, 22px voice on slides, short paragraphs. Front-load the point.
- **Stats:** let the number talk. Label in plain words underneath, no adjectives.
- **Quotes/testimonials:** verbatim, trimmed only for length, attributed with name and title.
- **Emails:** subject lines under 45 characters, preheader completes the subject rather than repeating it. Arabic emails follow the RTL rules in the email design system.
- **Social/LinkedIn:** first line earns the click alone (it is all people see collapsed). No hashtag walls; 3 maximum, at the end.

## Quick self-check before shipping copy

1. Could a competitor paste this over their logo? If yes, it is too generic; sharpen it.
2. Read it aloud. Anything you would not say to a client's face, rewrite.
3. Delete the first sentence if the second one is stronger without it. It usually is.
